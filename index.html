Projects

Acoustic Characterisation of Small Unmanned Aerial Vehicle Rotor Noise

Developed a Python-based signal processing pipeline to characterise propeller noise across an RPM sweep of 2250–5700, identifying blade passage frequency and SPL from averaged FFT spectra. Trained a linear regression model on spectral measurements to estimate rotor RPM, with extrapolation evaluated up to 10,000 RPM. Conducted at NIT Calicut in a semi-anechoic chamber using NI PXIe DAQ and PCB Piezotronics condenser microphone.

Comparative Analysis of Acoustic Barrier Insertion Loss

Developed a 2D FDTD simulation in Python for sound propagation over rigid ground; conducted field experiments over grassy ground. Key finding: experimental interference pattern showed systematic peak/dip inversion relative to simulation — consistent with ~180° phase shift from porous ground surface. Compared results against the Maekawa empirical curve; abstract submitted to IISA 2026 (under review).

Lombard Effect: Acoustic Signal Analysis of Noise-Induced Vocal Modulation

Designed a controlled experiment studying adaptive vocal modulation under varying ambient noise conditions. Performed spectral analysis — baseline-corrected SPL and fundamental frequency extraction — using Audacity and Python. Applied regression modelling to quantify the relationship between environmental noise level and vocal response.

Computational Modelling of Coupled Oscillatory Systems

Formulated equations of motion for multi-degree-of-freedom systems in matrix form; reduced dynamics to a generalised eigenvalue problem. Implemented numerical solvers in C++; analysed eigenfrequency spectra and mode structures under systematic parameter variation.

Acoustic Energy Harvesting: Literature-Based Computational Review

Reviewed and compared piezoelectric, electromagnetic, triboelectric, and metamaterial-based energy conversion mechanisms across published literature. Generated original Python plots comparing reported harvested power vs. SPL and operating frequency ranges across harvester architectures. Key finding: harvested power variability is dominated by experimental configuration rather than harvesting mechanism alone.
